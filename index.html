            <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-center mb-2">Diagnosis & Severity</h3>

            <p class="text-center text-gray-600 max-w-2xl mx-auto mb-8">ARDS diagnosis is based on the 2012 Berlin Definition, which requires four key criteria. This section provides an interactive way to explore these criteria and understand the severity classification based on oxygenation.</p>

            <div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto bg-white rounded-xl shadow-md overflow-hidden">

                <div class="p-6 md:p-8">

                    <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold mb-4">The Berlin Definition: Interactive Criteria</h4>

                    <div id="accordion-container" class="space-y-2">

                        <div class="accordion-item border rounded-lg">

                            <button class="accordion-header w-full text-left p-4 font-medium flex justify-between items-center transition duration-300 ease-in-out bg-gray-50 hover:bg-gray-100">

                                <span>1. Timing</span>

                                <span class="accordion-icon text-xl transform transition-transform duration-300">+</span>

                            </button>

                            <div class="accordion-content">

                                <p class="p-4 border-t text-gray-700">Acute onset within 1 week of a known clinical insult or new/worsening respiratory symptoms. This criterion helps distinguish ARDS from more chronic pulmonary conditions.</p>

                            </div>

                        </div>

                        <div class="accordion-item border rounded-lg">

                            <button class="accordion-header w-full text-left p-4 font-medium flex justify-between items-center transition duration-300 ease-in-out bg-gray-50 hover:bg-gray-100">

                                <span>2. Chest Imaging</span>

                                <span class="accordion-icon text-xl transform transition-transform duration-300">+</span>

                            </button>

                            <div class="accordion-content">

                                <p class="p-4 border-t text-gray-700">Bilateral opacities on chest radiograph or CT scan, not fully explained by effusions, lobar/lung collapse, or nodules. These opacities are consistent with pulmonary edema.</p>

                            </div>

                        </div>

                        <div class="accordion-item border rounded-lg">

                            <button class="accordion-header w-full text-left p-4 font-medium flex justify-between items-center transition duration-300 ease-in-out bg-gray-50 hover:bg-gray-100">

                                <span>3. Origin of Edema</span>

                                <span class="accordion-icon text-xl transform transition-transform duration-300">+</span>

                            </button>

                            <div class="accordion-content">

                                <p class="p-4 border-t text-gray-700">Respiratory failure not fully explained by cardiac failure or fluid overload. An objective assessment (e.g., echocardiogram) is needed to exclude hydrostatic edema if no risk factor is present.</p>

                            </div>

                        </div>

                        <div class="accordion-item border rounded-lg">

                            <button class="accordion-header w-full text-left p-4 font-medium flex justify-between items-center transition duration-300 ease-in-out bg-gray-50 hover:bg-gray-100">

                                <span>4. Oxygenation (Severity)</span>

                                <span class="accordion-icon text-xl transform transition-transform duration-300">+</span>

                            </button>

                            <div class="accordion-content">

                                <div class="p-4 border-t text-gray-700">

                                    <p class="mb-2">Impaired oxygenation is measured by the PaO₂/FiO₂ ratio on a minimum PEEP of ≥ 5 cmH₂O. This ratio determines the severity:</p>

                                    <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1">

                                        <li><span class="font-semibold">Mild:</span> 200 mmHg &lt; PaO₂/FiO₂ ≤ 300 mmHg</li>

                                        <li><span class="font-semibold">Moderate:</span> 100 mmHg &lt; PaO₂/FiO₂ ≤ 200 mmHg</li>

                                        <li><span class="font-semibold">Severe:</span> PaO₂/FiO₂ ≤ 100 mmHg</li>

                                    </ul>

                                </div>

                            </div>

            <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-center mb-2">Management Toolkit</h3>

            <p class="text-center text-gray-600 max-w-2xl mx-auto mb-8">ARDS management is primarily supportive, focusing on lung-protective ventilation and evidence-based adjunctive therapies. Use the tabs below to navigate the core components of ARDS care.</p>

                <div class="mb-4 flex justify-center border-b border-gray-200">

                    <div class="tab-content active" id="ventilation">

                        <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-md grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-6">

                            <div>

                                <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Low Tidal Volume Ventilation</h4>

                                <p class="text-gray-600">The cornerstone of ARDS care. Aims to prevent ventilator-induced lung injury (VILI) by avoiding overdistension of the "baby lung."</p>

                                <ul class="mt-2 list-disc list-inside text-gray-700">

                                    <li><span class="font-semibold">Tidal Volume:</span> 4-8 mL/kg predicted body weight.</li>

                                    <li><span class="font-semibold">Key Evidence:</span> ARDSNet trial showed a significant mortality reduction with 6 mL/kg vs 12 mL/kg.</li>

                                </ul>

                            </div>

                             <div>

                                <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Pressure Limitation & PEEP</h4>

                                <p class="text-gray-600">Essential for preventing barotrauma and atelectrauma (injury from repetitive alveolar collapse and reopening).</p>

                                <ul class="mt-2 list-disc list-inside text-gray-700">

                                    <li><span class="font-semibold">Plateau Pressure:</span> Goal is &lt; 30 cmH₂O.</li>

                                    <li><span class="font-semibold">PEEP:</span> Optimized to maintain alveolar recruitment while avoiding overdistension and hemodynamic compromise. Often guided by ARDSNet PEEP/FiO₂ tables.</li>

                                </ul>

                            </div>

                        </div>

                    </div>

                    <div class="tab-content hidden" id="adjunctive">

                         <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-md grid sm:grid-cols-2 lg:grid-cols-2 gap-6">

                            <div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">

                                <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Prone Positioning</h4>

                                <p class="text-gray-600 text-sm">Strongly recommended for severe ARDS (PaO₂/FiO₂ ≤ 100). Improves V/Q matching and reduces VILI. PROSEVA trial showed a significant mortality benefit when used for >12 hours/day.</p>

                            </div>

                            <div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">

                                <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Neuromuscular Blockers</h4>

                                <p class="text-gray-600 text-sm">Conditional recommendation for early, severe ARDS (PaO₂/FiO₂ &lt; 150) to improve patient-ventilator synchrony. Evidence is mixed (ACURASYS vs. ROSE trials).</p>

                            </div>

                            <div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">

                                <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Corticosteroids</h4>

                                <p class="text-gray-600 text-sm">Recent guidelines suggest use to mitigate the inflammatory response. Patient selection and timing are critical. Not a routine therapy for all ARDS patients.</p>

                            </div>

                            <div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">

                                <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">ECMO</h4>

                                <p class="text-gray-600 text-sm">A salvage therapy for refractory severe ARDS when conventional ventilation fails. Considered for patients with PaO₂/FiO₂ &lt; 80 despite optimization.</p>

                            </div>

                        </div>

                    </div>

                    <div class="tab-content hidden" id="supportive">

                        <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-md grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-6">

                            <div>

                                <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Conservative Fluid Management</h4>

                                <p class="text-gray-600">After initial resuscitation, a conservative fluid strategy is recommended. The FACTT trial showed this approach leads to more ventilator-free days and shorter ICU stays without increasing non-pulmonary organ failure.</p>

                            </div>

                             <div>

                                <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Nutritional Support</h4>

                                <p class="text-gray-600">Early enteral nutrition (within 24-48 hours) is crucial. Aim for 25-30 kcal/kg/day and higher protein intake (1.2-2.0 g/kg/day) to combat catabolism and support tissue repair.</p>

                            </div>

                        </div>

            <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-center mb-2">Prognosis & Life After ARDS</h3>

            <p class="text-center text-gray-600 max-w-2xl mx-auto mb-8">While mortality remains high, many patients survive ARDS. However, survivorship is often complicated by Post-Intensive Care Syndrome (PICS), a collection of long-term physical, cognitive, and mental health impairments.</p>

            <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 items-center">

                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-md text-center">

                    <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold mb-2">Mortality Rate</h4>

                    <p class="text-5xl font-bold text-[#E76F51]">25-46%</p>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Mortality varies based on severity, underlying etiology (sepsis-induced is worse), and patient comorbidities.</p>

                </div>

                    const metrics = ctx.measureText(testLine);

                    if (testWidth > maxWidth && n > 0) {

                        ctx.fillText(line, x, y);

                        y += lineHeight;

                    } else {

                        line = testLine;

                    }

                }

                ctx.fillText(line, x, y);

            };
